Problem 1811 - 2021 RMMSL, A4

Difficulty: 8

Let \(f: \mathbf{R} \to \mathbf{R}\) be a non-decreasing function such that \(f(y) - f(x) < y - x\) for all real numbers \(x\) and \(y > x\). The sequence \(u_1,u_2,\ldots\) of real numbers is such that \(u_{n+2} = f(u_{n+1}) - f(u_n)\) for all \(n\geq 1\). Prove that \(\lim_{n\to \infty} u_n = 0\).
