Problem 422 - 2002 France TST, P3 of 6

Difficulty: 6

Let \(n\) be a positive integer and let \((a_1, a_2, \dots , a_{2n})\) be a permutation of \(1, 2, \dots , 2n\) such that the numbers \(\lvert a_{i + 1} - a_i \rvert\) are pairwise distinct for \(i = 1, 2, \dots , 2n-1.\) Prove that \(\{ a_2, a_4, \dots , a_{2n} \} = \{1, 2, \dots , n\}\) if and only if \(a_1 - a_{2n} = n.\)
