Problem 1124 - 2012 IMOSL, A2

Difficulty: 7

Let \(\mathbf{Z}\) and \(\mathbf{Q}\) be the sets of integers and rationals respectively.

  1. Does there exist a partition of \(\mathbf{Z}\) into three non-empty subsets \(A\), \(B\), \(C\) such that the sets \(A+B\), \(B+C\), \(C+A\) are disjoint?
  2. Does there exist a partition of \(\mathbf{Q}\) into three non-empty subsets \(A\), \(B\), \(C\) such that the sets \(A+B\), \(B+C\), \(C+A\) are disjoint?

Here \(X+Y\) denotes the set \(\{ x+y \mid x \in X, y \in Y \}\), for \(X,Y \subseteq \mathbf{Z}\) and \(X,Y \subseteq \mathbf{Q}\).
