Problem 1805 - 2024 MODS Geometry Contest, P6 of 6, G

Difficulty: 9

Let \(\triangle ABC\) have orthocenter \(H\), circumcenter \(O\) and circumcircle \(\Gamma\). Let \(M\) be the midpoint of \(BC\), and let \(AM\) meet \(\Gamma\) at \(K \neq A\). Let \(BH\), \(CH\) meet \(AM\) at \(X\), \(Y\) and \(\Gamma\) at \(H_B \neq B\), \(H_C \neq C\). If \(BY\), \(CX\) meet \(\Gamma\) at \(B_1 \neq B\), \(C_1 \neq C\), show that \(B_1H_B, C_1H_C\) and \(OK\) concur or are pairwise parallel.
