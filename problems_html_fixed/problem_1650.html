Problem 1650 - 2023 Grossman MO, P7 of 7, GC

Difficulty: 7

The plane is colored with two colors so that the following property holds: for each real \(a>0\) there is an equilateral triangle in the plane of side length \(a\) whose \(3\) vertices are of the same color.

Prove that for any three numbers \(a,b,c>0\) for which the sum of any two is greater than the third there is a triangle in the plane with sides \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\) whose \(3\) vertices are of the same color.
