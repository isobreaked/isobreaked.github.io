Problem 1621 - 2020 MEMO Individual, P1 of 4

Difficulty: 6

Let \(\mathbf{N}\) be the set of positive integers. Determine all positive integers \(k\) for which there exist functions \(f : \mathbf{N} \to \mathbf{N}\) and \(g : \mathbf{N} \to \mathbf{N}\) such that \(g\) assumes infinitely many values and such that \[f^{g(n)}(n) = f(n) + k\] holds for every positive integer \(n\).
