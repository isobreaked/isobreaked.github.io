Problem 887 - 2020 IMOSL, N4, N

Difficulty: 8

For any odd prime \(p\) and any integer \(n\), let \(d_p (n) \in \{ 0,1, \dots, p-1 \}\) denote the remainder when \(n\) is divided by \(p\). We say that \((a_0, a_1, a_2, \dots)\) is a p-sequence, if \(a_0\) is a positive integer coprime to \(p,\) and \(a_{n+1} =a_n + d_p (a_n)\) for \(n \geqslant 0\).

  1. Do there exist infinitely many primes \(p\) for which there exist \(p\)-sequences (\(a_0\), \(a_1\), \(a_2\), \(\dots\)) and (\(b_0\), \(b_1\), \(b_2\), \(\dots\)) such that \(a_n >b_n\) for infinitely many \(n,\) and \(b_n > a_n\) for infinitely many \(n\)?
  2. Do there exist infinitely many primes \(p\) for which there exist \(p\)-sequences (\(a_0\), \(a_1\), \(a_2\), \(\dots\)) and (\(b_0\), \(b_1\), \(b_2\), \(\dots\)) such that \(a_0 < b_0,\) but \(a_n > b_n\) for all \(n \geqslant 1\)?
