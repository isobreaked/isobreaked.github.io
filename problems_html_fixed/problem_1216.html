Problem 1216 - 2022 Israel Olympic Revenge, P2 of 4, N

Difficulty: 8

A triple \((a,b,c)\) of positive integers is called \(\textbf{strong}\) if the following holds: for every integer \(m>1\), the number \(a+b+c\) does not divide \(a^m+b^m+c^m\). The \(\textbf{sum}\) of a strong triple \((a,b,c)\) is defined as \(a+b+c\).

Prove that there exists an infinite set of strong triples with pairwise coprime sums.
