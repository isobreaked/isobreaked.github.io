Problem 2062 - Original

Difficulty: 6

A game is played between two players, \(A\) and \(B\). First \(A\) picks a directed graph with at least \(2\) vertices such that if \(u\) is connected to \(v\) then \(v\) isn't connected to \(u\). Then \(B\) picks the starting positions for \(A\) and \(B\), which must be in different positions. Now the game starts on \(A\)'s move, and then alternates between them. On \(A\)'s move, if she is at a vertex \(v\) with a path from \(u\) to \(v\), she can move to \(u\). If she has no legal moves, she loses. On \(B\)'s turn, he moves along a path (but in the right direction). If he has no legal moves he stays in place. If \(A\) and \(B\) are ever in the same vertex, \(B\) wins. If A never gets caught, she wins. Who wins?
