Problem 635 - 2016 USA TST, P3 of 6

Difficulty: 8

Let \(p\) be a prime number. Let \(\mathbf F_p\) denote the integers modulo \(p\), and let \(\mathbf F_p[x]\) be the set of polynomials with coefficients in \(\mathbf F_p\). Define \(\Psi : \mathbf F_p[x] \to \mathbf F_p[x]\) by\[\Psi\left( \sum_{i=0}^n a_i x^i \right) = \sum_{i=0}^n a_i x^{p^i}.\]Prove that for nonzero polynomials \(F,G \in \mathbf F_p[x]\),\[\Psi(\gcd(F,G)) = \gcd(\Psi(F), \Psi(G)).\]Here, a polynomial \(Q\) divides \(P\) if there exists \(R \in \mathbf F_p[x]\) such that \(P(x) - Q(x) R(x)\) is the polynomial with all coefficients \(0\) (with all addition and multiplication in the coefficients taken modulo \(p\)), and the gcd of two polynomials is the highest degree polynomial with leading coefficient \(1\) which divides both of them. A non-zero polynomial is a polynomial with not all coefficients \(0\). As an example of multiplication, \((x+1)(x+2)(x+3) = x^3+x^2+x+1\) in \(\mathbf F_5[x]\).
