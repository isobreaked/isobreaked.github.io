Problem 1279 - 2022 USAMO, P5 of 6

Difficulty: 8

A function \(f: \mathbf{R}\to \mathbf{R}\) is essentially increasing if \(f(s)\leq f(t)\) holds whenever \(s\leq t\) are real numbers such that \(f(s)\neq 0\) and \(f(t)\neq 0\). Find the smallest integer \(k\) such that for any 2022 real numbers \(x_1,x_2,\ldots , x_{2022},\) there exist \(k\) essentially increasing functions \(f_1,\ldots, f_k\) such that \[f_1(n) + f_2(n) + \cdots + f_k(n) = x_n\qquad \text{for every } n= 1,2,\ldots 2022.\]
