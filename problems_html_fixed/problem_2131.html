Problem 2131 - 2015 IMOSL, N2, N

Difficulty: 6

Let \(a\) and \(b\) be positive integers such that \(a! + b!\) divides \(a!b!\). Prove that \(3a \ge 2b + 2\).

Idea

The really nice idea to size-bound this divisibility is to find a number that divides the RHS and yet is relatively prime to what we already know divides the RHS. I’ll call this Nukelid’s Algorithm.
