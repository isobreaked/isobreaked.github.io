Problem 662 - West Australian Geometry Olympiad, P3 of 4 , G

Difficulty: 9

Let \(ABC\) be a triangle and \(\omega\) its circumcircle. Denote by \(I\) the incentre and \(O\) the circumcentre. Let the \(A\)-mixtilinear incircle be internally tangent to \(\omega\) at \(T\), and let \(D\), \(E\) and \(F\) be the feet of the internal angle bisectors from \(A\), \(B\) and \(C\) respectively. Suppose that \(AT\) meets \(EF\) at \(P\). If \(I\), \(P\) and \(O\) are collinear, then prove that \(O\), \(D\) and \(T\) are also collinear.
