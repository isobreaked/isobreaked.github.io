Problem 1797 - 2023 IMC, P4 of 10 (adapted) , N

Difficulty: 7

Let \(p>2\) be a prime number and let \(k\) be a positive integer. Suppose that the numbers \(a_i=i^k+i\) for \(i=0,1, \ldots,p-1\) leave pairwise distinct remainders upon division by \(p\). What is the set of possible remainders of \(a_2\) upon division by \(p\)?

Thinking

The distint remainders condition means that for any \(i\), \(j\), \[i^k + i \not \equiv j^k + j \pmod{p}\] Of course, taking \(i = 0\) and \(1\), we observe that \(0\) and \(2\) are off limits: \[i^k \not \equiv -i \pmod{p}\] \[i^k \not \equiv 2 - i \pmod{p}\] Note that \(k\) is odd, if otherwise then \((p - 1)^k + (p - 1) \equiv 0 \pmod{p}\). Thus the value for \(p - 1\) is \(-2\), and all the values from \((p + 1)/2\) to \(p - 1\) are just negations of the values from \(1\) to \((p - 1)/2\).
