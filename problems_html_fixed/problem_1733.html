Given are two positive integers \(n,k\) with \(n>2\) a multiple of \(k\). Define the invertibility of a permutation \(A=(a_1,a_2,\dots,a_n)\) of \(\{1,2,\dots,n\}\) to be \[I(A)=\sum_{\substack{i < j \\ a_i > a_j}} (a_i-a_j).\] Call such a permutation \(A\) \(k\)-miraculous if \(k \mid I(A)\). Show that the number of \(k\)-miraculous permutations is divisible by \(k\).
