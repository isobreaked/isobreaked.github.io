Problem 1930 - 2024 Singapore MO Open, P5 of 5, CN

Difficulty: 7

For each prime \(p\), find the largest positive integer \(N\) such that it is possible to fill an \(N \times N\) table with integers such that for any four cells forming a lattice rectangle, the products of the two numbers on both pairs of opposite corners are distinct in mod \(p\). That is, if we let \(a_{rc}\) be the number on the cell located in the \(r^{th}\) row and \(c^{th}\) column then \(a_{ik} a_{jl} - a_{il} a_{jk}\) is never divisible by \(p\).
