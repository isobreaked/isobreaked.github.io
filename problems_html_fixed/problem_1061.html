Problem 1061 - 2022 BMO2, P4 of 4, G

Difficulty: 6

Let \(ABC\) be an acute angled triangle with circumcircle \(\Gamma\). Let \(\ell_B\) and \(\ell_C\) be the lines perpendicular to \(BC\) which pass through \(B\) and \(C\) respectively. A point \(T\) lies on the minor arc \(BC\). The tangent to \(\Gamma\) at \(T\) meets \(\ell_B\) and \(\ell_C\) at \(P_B\) and \(P_C\) respectively. The line through \(P_B\) perpendicular to \(AC\) and the line through \(P_C\) perpendicular to \(AB\) meet at a point \(Q\). Given that \(Q\) lies on \(BC\), prove that the line \(AT\) passes through \(Q\).
