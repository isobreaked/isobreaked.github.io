Problem 2279 - 2025 Iran TST, P5 of 12, G

Difficulty: 8

In a scalene triangle \(ABC\), \(D\) is the point of tangency of the incircle with the side \(BC\). Points \(T_B\) and \(T_C\) are the intersections of the angle bisectors of \(\angle ABC\) and \(\angle ACB\) with the circumcircle of \(ABC\), respectively. Let \(X_B\) be the antipodal point of \(A\) in the circumcircle of \(ACD\), and let \(X_C\) be the antipodal point of \(A\) in the circumcircle of \(ABD\). Prove that triangles \(B T_C X_C\) and \(C T_B X_B\) are similar.
