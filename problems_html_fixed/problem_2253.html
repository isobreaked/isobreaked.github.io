Problem 2253 - 2025 Sauna Final Round, P3 of 3, G

Difficulty: 8

Let \(ABC\) be a acute triangle and \(E\), \(F\) are points on \(AB\) and \(AC\) respectively such that \(E,F,C,B\) lie on one circle. Let \(P\) be the intersection of \(BF\) and \(CE\). Let \(M\) and \(N\) be the midpoints of \(BC\) and \(AP\) respectively. Let \(X\) be the normal projection of \(P\) onto \(AB\).

Prove that \(\angle MXN + \angle BFC=180^\circ\)

Thinking (Mock)

This is quadrilateral config, not triangle config. Actually wait, in terms of triangle config, this is generalized NPC. Note that

  • \(NX = NY\) since circle \((APXY)\) has center \(N\).
  • \(MX = MY\) due to complex bash.

Since we wish to show \(\angle MXN = \angle MYN = 180^{\circ} - \angle BFC\), we want \[\angle XMY = 360^{\circ} - (360^{\circ} - 2\angle BFC) - \angle XNY = 2 \angle BFC - 2 \angle BAC = 2 \angle EBF\] So we can remove \(A\) and \(N\) from the problem, and restate in terms of a pure quadrilateral problem. But also note that if \(Z\) is the foot of \(P\) onto \(BC\), then we know \(\angle XZY = \angle XBP + \angle PCY = 2 \angle EBF\). So really what we want to show is that \(X\), \(Y\), \(Z\), \(M\) are concyclic. Since \(ZP\) is the internal angle bisector of \(\angle XZY\), the line \(ZM\) must be the external angle bisector. Hence, the point \(M'\) where \(XM\) intersects the circle \((XYZ)\) must lie on the perpendicular bisector of \(XY\). Since \(MX = MY\), \(M' = M\) must hold, and we are done.

Idea

Just think forward+backward and metagame from the asymmetric problem statement.
