Problem 327 - Chevalley's Theorem

Difficulty: 9

Let \(P_1(x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n)\), \(P_2\)(\(x_1\), \(x_2\), \dots, \(x_n\)), \(\dots\), \(P_r(x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n)\) be polynomials with integer coefficients modulo \(p\). Suppose further that \(n\) is greater than the sum of the degrees. Prove that if \(x_1 = 0\), \(x_2 = 0\), \(\dots\), \(x_n = 0\) is a root of all the polynomials modulo \(p\), then there exists another common root modulo \(p\).
