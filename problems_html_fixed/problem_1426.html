Problem 1426 - 2022 CGMO, P8 of 8

Difficulty: 8

Let \(x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_{11}\) be nonnegative reals such that their sum is \(1\). For \(i = 1,2, \ldots, 11\), let \[y_i = \begin{cases} x_{i} + x_{i + 1}, & i \text{ odd} ,\\ x_{i} + x_{i + 1} + x_{i + 2}, & i \text{ even} \end{cases}\] where \(x_{12} = x_{1}\). And let \(F (x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_{11}) = y_1 y_2 \ldots y_{11}\). Prove that \(x_6 < x_8\) when \(F\) achieves its maximum.
