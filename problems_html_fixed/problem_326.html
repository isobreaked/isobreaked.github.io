Problem 326 - 2013 IMO, P5 of 6

Difficulty: 8

Let \(\mathbf{Q}_{>0}\) be the set of all positive rational numbers. Let \({f} : {\mathbf{Q}_{>0}} \to {\mathbf{R}}\) be a function satisfying the following three conditions:

  1. for all \(x,y \in \mathbf{Q}_{>0}\), we have \(f(x)f(y) \geq f(xy)\);
  2. for all \(x,y \in \mathbf{Q}_{>0}\), we have \(f(x+y) \geq f(x)+f(y)\);
  3. there exists a rational number \(a> 1\) such that \(f(a)=a\).
Prove that \(f(x) = x\) for all \(x \in \mathbf{Q}_{>0}\).
