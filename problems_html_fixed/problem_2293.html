Problem 2293 - Amateurs Geometry Olympiad 3, P2 of 6, G

Difficulty: 7

Let \(ABCD\) be a parallelogram and \(E\) a point on \(AD\) such that \(\angle ABE = \angle DBC\). Let \(O_A\) and \(O_C\) be the circumcenters of \(\triangle ABE\) and \(\triangle DBC\) respectively. Lines \(AO_C\) and \(CO_A\) meet at \(K\).

Prove that \(DC \perp BK\).

Thinking

We know \((BA, BC)\), \((BE, BD)\), \((BO_A, BO_C)\) are pairs of an involution. Due to DDIT with quadrilateral \(AO_ACO_C\) it suffices to show that if \(X = AO_A \cap CO_C\), then \(XB \perp BC\). But the point on \(CO_C\) such that this holds is the antipode of \(C\) in \((BCD)\).

So all we need to show is that \(AO_A\) passes through the antipode of \(C\) in \((BCD)\). Call this point \(X'\). But surely this is just angles - note that \(\angle O_A A E = 90^{\circ} - \angle A B E = 90^{\circ} - \angle CBD = 90^{\circ} - \angle ADB\). But wait - \(A\) is the reflection of \(C\) in the midpoint of \(BD\) (call this \(M\)), \(X'\) is the reflection of \(C\) in \(O_C\). This means that \(X'A \parallel MO_C \perp BD\), which indeed means exactly that \(\angle X'AD = 90^{\circ} + \angle ADB\), which is what we wanted.

Idea

Angle bisectors and right angles made me think of projective for a second but then DDIT just shows up and destroys the problem.
