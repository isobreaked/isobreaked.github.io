Problem 2203 - 2016 RMM, P4 of 6

Difficulty: 7

Let \(x\) and \(y\) be positive real numbers such that \(x + y^{2016} \ge 1\). Prove that \(x^{2016} + y > 0.99\).

Thinking

Note that if \(y < 0.99\), then \(y^{2016} < 0.0000000025\), which means \(x > 0.9999999\), implying \(x^{2016} > 0.999\). What? Is the point supposed to be computation? I used a calculator already.

I suppose on contest you want to show \[(1 - (0.99)^{2016})^{2016} > 0.99\] without a calculator. Use Bernoulli's inequality or whatever they call it to want \(1 - 2016 \cdot 0.99^{2016} > 0.99\), so we want \[0.99^{2016} < 0.000005 \iff 99^{2016} < 100^{2013} \cdot 5 = 5 (99^{2013} + 99^{2012} (2013) + 99^{2011} \binom{2013}{2} + \dots + 99^2 \binom{2013}{2011} + 99 \binom{2013}{2012} + 1)\] But it is in fact true that \[99^{2016} < 5 \cdot \binom{2013}{7} 99^{2006} \iff 99^{10} < 5 \cdot \binom{2013}{7} \impliedby 100^{10} \cdot 5040 < 5 \cdot (2000)^7 \iff 10^{20} \cdot 5040 < 5 \cdot 2^7 \cdot 10^{21} \iff 504 < 640\] so we are done.
