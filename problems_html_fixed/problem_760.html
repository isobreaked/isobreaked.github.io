Problem 760 - 2020 Cono Sur Olympiad, P3 of 6, G

Difficulty: 7

Let \(A B C\) be an acute triangle with circumcircle \(\omega\) such that \(A C < B C\), and let \(M\) be the midpoint of \(B C\). Points \(F\) and \(E\) are chosen on \(A B\) and \(B C\) respectively, such that \(A C=C F\) and \(E B=E F\). The line \(A M\) intersects \(\omega\) again at \(D\).

Prove that the lines \(D E\) and \(F M\) intersect on \(\omega\).

Idea

A lot of stuff is motivated by the desire to use ratio lemma - but unfortunately, directly using ratio lemma is impossible because \(F\) is a random point in the middle of nowhere. So we try to characterize \(F\) and note that it lies on \((HBC)\), which is interesting, and stumble upon \(F'\) - which can also directly be motivated by ratio lemma - after all, we wanted some sort of sidekick of \(F\) that lies on \((ABC)\) in the first place.
