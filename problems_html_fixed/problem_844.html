Difficulty: 6

Let \(S\) be the set of all infinite positive integer sequences. We say that two sequences in \(S\) are separated if, for all positive integers \(n\), the \(n\)th term in the first sequence is distinct from the \(n\)th term in the second sequence.

Find all functions \(f:S\to \mathbf{Z}^+\) such that:

  1. \(f(s_1)\neq f(s_2)\) for any two separated sequences \(s_1,s_2\in S\);
  2. For any constant sequence \(s=(c,c,c,\dots)\in S\), we have \(f(s)=c\).
