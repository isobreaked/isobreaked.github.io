Problem 1215 - 2015 RMM, P4 of 6, G

Difficulty: 6

Let \(ABC\) be a triangle, and let \(D\) be the point where the incircle meets side \(BC\). Let \(J_b\) and \(J_c\) be the incentres of the triangles \(ABD\) and \(ACD\), respectively. Prove that the circumcentre of the triangle \(AJ_bJ_c\) lies on the angle bisector of \(\angle BAC\).

Thinking

We first see that \(J_bJ_c \perp AD\) by touchpoint lengths. Since we want to show the circumcenter lies on a line, we will obviously do the standard reflect point about line trick - let \(J_b'\) be the reflection of \(J_b\) about \(AI\), where \(I\) is the incenter of \(ABC\). Now, since \(AJ_b = AJ_b'\), using \(J_bJ_c\perp AD\) we are just an angle chase away: \[\angle AJ_b'J_b = 90^{\circ} - \angle CAD/2 =\angle AJ_cJ_b\]

Idea

You get a lot of information very very quickly - the main job in these problems is to just piece it together and realize when you have enough info.
