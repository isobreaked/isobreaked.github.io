Problem 971 - 2019 China TST Test 3, P3 of 6

Difficulty: 8

Does there exist a bijection \(f:\mathbf{N}^{+} \rightarrow \mathbf{N}^{+}\), such that there exist a positive integer \(k\), and it's possible to have each positive integer colored by one of \(k\) chosen colors, such that for any \(x \neq y\) , \(f(x)+y\) and \(f(y)+x\) are not the same color?
