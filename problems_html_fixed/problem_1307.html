Problem 1307 - 2004 IMC, Day 1 P2 of 6

Difficulty: 6

Let \(P(x)=x^2-1\). How many distinct real solutions does the following equation have: \[\underbrace{P(P(\cdots (P}_{2004}(x))\cdots ))=0\ ?\]

Thinking

Small cases \[x^2 - 1 = 0 \implies x \in \{1, -1\}\] \[x^2 - 1 \in \{1, -1\} \implies x \in \left\{\sqrt{2}, -\sqrt{2}, 0\right\}\] \[x^2 - 1 \in \{\sqrt{2}, -\sqrt{2}, 0\} \implies x \in \left\{\sqrt{\sqrt{2} + 1}, -\sqrt{\sqrt{2} + 1}, 1, -1\right\}\] \[x^2 - 1\in \left\{\sqrt{\sqrt{2} + 1}, -\sqrt{\sqrt{2} + 1}, 1, -1\right\} \implies x \in \left\{\sqrt{\sqrt{\sqrt{2} +1} + 1}, -\sqrt{\sqrt{\sqrt{2} +1} + 1}, \sqrt{2}, -\sqrt{2}, 0 \right\}\] \[x^2 - 1 \in \left\{\sqrt{\sqrt{\sqrt{2} +1} + 1}, -\sqrt{\sqrt{\sqrt{2} +1} + 1}, \sqrt{2}, -\sqrt{2}, 0 \right\} \]\[\implies x \in \left\{\sqrt{\sqrt{\sqrt{\sqrt{2} +1} + 1} + 1}, -\sqrt{\sqrt{\sqrt{\sqrt{2} +1} + 1} + 1}, \sqrt{\sqrt{2} + 1}, -\sqrt{\sqrt{2} + 1}, 1, -1 \right\}\] Okay this is fairly simple induction - answer is \(2003\).
