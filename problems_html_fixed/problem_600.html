Problem 600 - 2019 IMOSL, N6, N

Difficulty: 8

Let \(H = \{ \lfloor i\sqrt{2}\rfloor : i \in \mathbf Z_{>0}\} = \{1,2,4,5,7,\dots \}\) and let \(n\) be a positive integer. Prove that there exists a constant \(C\) such that, if \(A\subseteq \{1,2,\dots, n\}\) satisfies \(|A| \ge C\sqrt{n}\), then there exist \(a,b\in A\) such that \(a-b\in H\). (Here \(\mathbf Z_{>0}\) is the set of positive integers, and \(\lfloor z\rfloor\) denotes the greatest integer less than or equal to \(z\).)
