Problem 1679 - 2023 ELMOSL, C7

Difficulty: 9

A discrete hexagon with center \((a,b,c)\) (where \(a\), \(b\), \(c\) are integers) and radius \(r\) (a nonnegative integer) is the set of lattice points \((x,y,z)\) such that \(x+y+z=a+b+c\) and \(\max (|x-a|, |y-b|, |z-c|)\leq r\). Let \(n\) be a nonnegative integer and \(S\) be the set of triples \((x,y,z)\) of nonnegative integers such that \(x+y+z=n\). If \(S\) is partitioned into discrete hexagons, show that at least \(n+1\) hexagons are needed.
