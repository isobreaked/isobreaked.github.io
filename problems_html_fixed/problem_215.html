Problem 215 - 2017 IGO Advanced, P5 of 5 , G

Difficulty: 9

Sphere \(S\) touches a plane. Let \(A, B, C, D\) be four points on this plane such that no three of them are collinear. Consider the point \(A'\) such that S is tangent to the faces of the tetrahedron \(A'BCD.\) Points \(B', C', D'\) are defined similarly. Prove that \(A', B', C', D'\) are coplanar and the plane \(A'B'C'D'\) touches \(S.\)
