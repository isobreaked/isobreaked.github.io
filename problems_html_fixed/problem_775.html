Problem 775 - 2020 USA TSTST, P5 of 9

Difficulty: 7

Let \(\mathbf{N}^2\) denote the set of ordered pairs of positive integers. A finite subset \(S\) of \(\mathbf{N}^2\) is stable if whenever \((x,y)\) is in \(S\), then so are all points \((x',y')\) of \(\mathbf{N}^2\) with both \(x'\leq x\) and \(y'\leq y\). Prove that if \(S\) is a stable set, then among all stable subsets of \(S\) (including the empty set and \(S\) itself), at least half of them have an even number of elements.
