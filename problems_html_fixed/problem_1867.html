Problem 1867 - 2022 ``Blish'' Summer Competition, Intermediate Level P1 of 5, CN

Difficulty: 6

The expression

\[\frac?? + \frac?? + \dots + \frac??\]

is written on the board (in all 1000 such fractions). Derivative and Integral, in turn, replace one of the question marks with a positive integer that was not yet written on the board. Derivative starts and doesn't want the final expression to be an integer, while Integral does.

Who has a winning strategy?
